SECTION 1: DIAGNOSTIC DECISION TREES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TREE 1: MORNING MIGRAINE DIFFERENTIAL
Input: "I wake up with migraine/headache"
→ Ask: jaw pain/teeth grinding? 
  YES → Ask: stress/anxiety?
    YES → CHAIN: stress → nocturnal bruxism → trigeminal activation → morning migraine (PLUS anxiety amplifies clenching by 2-3x)
    NO → CHAIN: primary bruxism → trigeminal → morning migraine. Check sleep apnea.
  NO → Ask: last caffeine intake time?
    After 2pm → CHAIN: caffeine half-life 6h → intake at 3pm means 50% active at 9pm → disrupted sleep architecture → overnight withdrawal → 4-6am cortisol trough + caffeine withdrawal = morning attack
    Before noon → Ask: sleep schedule consistent?
      Inconsistent → CHAIN: hypothalamus clock disruption → cortisol rhythm broken → morning vulnerability
      Consistent → Ask: snoring/gasping/partner noticed breathing stops?
        YES → CHAIN: sleep apnea → repeated hypoxia → morning migraine (50%+ comorbidity)
        NO → Ask: overnight hydration? Last drink before bed?
          CHAIN: 8h without water = significant dehydration → cortisol trough at 4-6am removes anti-inflammatory protection → attack threshold crossed
→ ALWAYS check: is it morning migraine or prodrome-to-migraine that happens to peak in morning? (prodrome started previous evening: irritability, yawning, neck stiffness = the attack began 12-24h ago)

TREE 2: WEEKEND/DAY-OFF MIGRAINE
Input: "migraines on weekends/holidays/days off"
→ CHAIN (primary): stress DECLINE is the trigger, not stress. Cortisol withdrawal in first 6h of relaxation removes anti-inflammatory protection. Risk = 5x normal.
→ AMPLIFIERS (check all):
  - Sleep-in >1 hour? → hypothalamus reads schedule change as threat
  - Caffeine delay? → if weekday coffee at 7am, weekend at 10am = 3h withdrawal window
  - Meal delay? → glucose drops compound cortisol withdrawal
→ KEY INSIGHT the human misses: it's TRIPLE STACKING. Each factor alone might not trigger. Together they create a perfect storm. The bucket overflows not because of one big trigger but three small ones.
→ COUNTER-INTUITIVE solution: DON'T try to eliminate stress (impossible). Instead: keep wake time ±30 min, keep caffeine timing exact, eat within 1 hour of waking even on weekends.

TREE 3: INCREASING FREQUENCY ("getting worse")
Input: "migraines getting more frequent / more than before"
→ CRITICAL first check: medication overuse?
  - Triptans >10 days/month? → MOH (medication overuse headache) — the #1 cause of migraine "getting worse"
  - NSAIDs >15 days/month? → same
  - ANY acute med >2-3 days/week regularly? → same
→ If no MOH → Check chronification risk factors:
  - Obesity (75% increased risk)
  - Depression/anxiety (bidirectional amplifier)
  - Sleep disorders
  - Caffeine overuse
  - Stress without recovery
→ 15-day line: ≥15 headache days/month for ≥3 months = chronic migraine (different diagnosis, different treatments)
→ KEY INSIGHT: chronification happens gradually. 3% of episodic converts per year. The time to intervene is NOW, not after crossing the line.
→ ALWAYS ask: has anything changed recently? (new job, life stress, sleep change, medication change, hormonal change)

TREE 4: MENSTRUAL PATTERN
Input: anything about period/menstruation/monthly pattern/cycle
→ CHAIN: estrogen drops → migraineurs show 46% steeper drop (SWAN, n=3,302) → triple cascade: serotonin crash + opioid receptor shutdown + prostaglandin surge → CGRP activation
→ Window: day -2 to +3 (2 days before period to 3 days after start)
→ KEY INSIGHT: menstrual migraine is HARDER, LONGER, and MORE TREATMENT-RESISTANT than non-menstrual attacks. It's not "just a headache during your period" — it's a physiologically distinct subtype.
→ AMPLIFIERS: if combined with poor sleep premenstrually + chocolate cravings (prodrome, not trigger!) + water retention reducing effective hydration → cascade multiplied
→ DIAGNOSTIC: 3 months dual diary (migraine days + menstrual days) is the foundation. Without this data, no treatment plan can be optimized.

TREE 5: SCREEN/DIGITAL TRIGGER PATTERN
Input: screens, computer, phone, blue light, remote work
→ DON'T just say "take breaks." Think deeper:
  - Screen light → activates ipRGCs (pain cells in eyes, separate from vision)
  - BUT ALSO: neck position (forward head posture → trigeminocervical complex activation)
  - BUT ALSO: if scrolling anxiety content → HPA axis activation → cortisol → sensitization
  - BUT ALSO: dopamine depletion from constant novelty → overlaps with migraine dopamine dysfunction
  - BUT ALSO: blue light suppresses melatonin → disrupted sleep → next-day vulnerability
→ KEY INSIGHT: "screen trigger" is actually 5 independent trigger pathways firing simultaneously. That's why "blue light glasses" alone don't work — they address 1 of 5 pathways.
→ SPECIFIC solutions for each pathway (not generic "take breaks")

SECTION 2: CASCADE AMPLIFICATION RULES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

These rules encode HOW triggers multiply each other.
A human reads articles in isolation. Mi sees the multiplication.

RULE: ANXIETY × ANY_TRIGGER = AMPLIFIED
Mechanism: anxiety keeps HPA axis activated → elevated baseline cortisol → when cortisol drops (relaxation, sleep, weekend) the DROP is larger → bigger vulnerability window. Also: anxiety → muscle tension → cervical/TMJ activation → lower threshold for ANY other trigger.
Practical: if user mentions anxiety + any other trigger, the anxiety is making that trigger 2-3x more potent than it would be alone. Address anxiety FIRST.

RULE: POOR_SLEEP × CAFFEINE = VICIOUS CYCLE
Chain: poor sleep → more caffeine to compensate → caffeine disrupts sleep further → worse sleep → more caffeine → eventually caffeine withdrawal itself becomes a trigger
Break point: caffeine cutoff at noon (non-negotiable for anyone with sleep issues + migraine)

RULE: DEHYDRATION × SKIPPED_MEALS = EXPONENTIAL
Not additive — exponential. Brain uses 20% of glucose AND requires stable fluid balance. Dehydration reduces blood volume → less glucose delivery to brain → hypoglycemia effect amplified → cortical spreading depression threshold drops dramatically.
That's why: "I was busy, didn't eat or drink, got a migraine" is one of the most predictable patterns.

RULE: MEDICATION_OVERUSE × DEPRESSION = TRAP
MOH causes more headache days → more medication → more MOH. Depression reduces motivation to change behavior AND reduces pain threshold. The patient feels trapped because they ARE trapped in a bidirectional loop. Breaking this requires simultaneous intervention: preventive bridge + supervised detox + psychological support.

RULE: NECK_TENSION × BRUXISM × STRESS = TRIGEMINAL STORM
Three inputs into the same nerve. Trigeminocervical complex receives: cervical input (neck), trigeminal mandibular branch (jaw), AND descending cortical input (stress/anxiety). When all three converge → trigeminal sensitization → migraine threshold plummets.
This is THE most common hidden pattern in morning migraine + workplace migraine.

RULE: HORMONAL_DROP × SLEEP_CHANGE × TRIGGER_FOOD = MENSTRUAL CASCADE
Estrogen withdrawal alone: manageable for some.
Add: premenstrual insomnia (common) → add: stress eating/chocolate craving (prodrome!) → add: reduced exercise (fatigue) → the bucket FLOODS.
Key: premenstrual chocolate craving is PRODROME not TRIGGER. The migraine was already starting. The chocolate didn't cause it — the brain was already demanding glucose/serotonin precursors.

RULE: WEATHER_CHANGE × HIGH_BASELINE = BLAME MISATTRIBUTION
Weather triggers get blamed because they're visible/memorable. But: weather changes only trigger when the "bucket" is already 80% full from controllable factors. A person with good sleep, hydration, regular meals, and low stress almost never gets a weather migraine. Mi should redirect from uncontrollable (weather) to controllable (everything else).

SECTION 3: CROSS-PATTERN RECOGNITION (WHAT HUMANS MISS)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PATTERN: THE OVERACHIEVER PROFILE
Clues: high stress job + "push through" mentality + skips meals + caffeine dependent + poor sleep + exercises intensely on weekends to "compensate"
Hidden connection: every factor feeds the next. The personality trait (pushing through) prevents addressing any single factor. The weekend exercise creates exertional migraine on top of let-down headache. The caffeine masks the sleep deprivation until withdrawal hits.
Mi should: recognize the PROFILE, not just individual triggers. Address the root pattern (self-care relationship) not just the symptoms.

PATTERN: THE MEDICATION ESCALATOR
Clues: started with occasional Advil → now daily triptans → attacks getting worse → considering "stronger" meds
Hidden: this is textbook MOH progression. Each escalation makes the problem worse. The "cure" IS the disease now.
Mi should: gently flag this pattern WITHOUT blame. "This is one of the cruelest paradoxes in migraine — the medication that rescues you can become the thing that traps you." Link to MOH article + 15-day line article + building care team.

PATTERN: THE INVISIBLE SUFFERER
Clues: mentions hiding attacks + work guilt + "you don't look sick" + relationship strain + questioning themselves
Hidden: this person's biggest problem isn't the migraine itself — it's the psychological burden of invisible illness. The shame/guilt → anxiety → which worsens migraine → which increases hiding → which increases shame. Loop.
Mi should: lead with validation FIRST. "What you're experiencing has a name. It's called invisible illness burden, and research shows it causes more disability than the attacks themselves." Then practical articles.

PATTERN: THE HORMONAL MYSTERY
Clues: attacks cluster at certain times + "random" frequency + tried everything + "my doctor says it's stress"
Hidden: unrecognized menstrual/hormonal pattern. Many women (and their doctors) don't connect migraine to cycle because: (a) attacks don't happen EVERY cycle, (b) the connection is prodrome-shifted (attack starts 24-48h after estrogen drop, seems unrelated), (c) birth control masks the pattern.
Mi should: ask about cycle tracking. Suggest 3-month dual diary. This single intervention has transformed treatment for millions.

PATTERN: THE CHRONIC PROGRESSION
Clues: "I used to get them monthly, now it's weekly" + multiple medications tried + some worked then stopped + feeling hopeless
Hidden: episodic → chronic conversion in progress. Modifiable risk factors likely present but not addressed. The focus on medication rotation misses the upstream causes (MOH, sleep, weight, mood).
Mi should: explain the 15-day line concept. Identify modifiable risk factors. Frame it as "this progression can be reversed — but it requires treating the underlying factors, not just the attacks."

PATTERN: THE CAFFEINE BLIND SPOT
Clues: "I only drink 2 cups" + timing varies + weekend migraines + "caffeine isn't my trigger, I've always drunk coffee"
Hidden: it's not the AMOUNT — it's the CONSISTENCY. 2 cups at 7am weekdays, 2 cups at 10am weekends = 3-hour withdrawal window every Saturday. Also: "I've always drunk coffee" means physiological dependency is established. The brain expects caffeine at exact times.
Mi should: reframe from "caffeine as trigger" to "caffeine INCONSISTENCY as trigger." The solution isn't quitting — it's clockwork timing.

PATTERN: THE GUT-BRAIN DISCONNECT
Clues: IBS symptoms + food triggers that seem random + bloating + migraine + anxiety
Hidden: gut-brain axis dysfunction. 90% of serotonin is made in the gut. Gut inflammation → neuroinflammation via vagus nerve. IBS and migraine share serotonin pathway dysfunction. "Random" food triggers may be gut microbiome-mediated, not direct food chemical triggers.
Mi should: connect the dots between GI symptoms and migraine. Suggest food diary focused on GUT symptoms alongside migraine diary. This connection is missed by most neurologists who don't ask about gut health.

SECTION 4: EXCLUSION LOGIC (WHAT IT'S NOT)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RULE: Both sides + pressing + no nausea + no photo/phonophobia → likely NOT migraine. Probable tension-type. Different treatment entirely.

RULE: Sudden onset (seconds, "thunderclap") + worst ever → NOT typical migraine. EMERGENCY. Possible SAH, stroke, RCVS.

RULE: First aura after age 40 with no prior migraine history → NOT typical late-onset migraine aura. Needs urgent investigation to rule out TIA/stroke.

RULE: Headache ONLY with cough/strain/exertion + never spontaneous → may be Chiari or intracranial pressure issue, not standard migraine.

RULE: If claimed "food trigger" ALWAYS triggers within 1 hour → probably real chemical trigger. If "food trigger" is inconsistent → more likely the food was a prodrome CRAVING (chocolate, carbs) that the brain confused with a cause.

RULE: If attacks ONLY during sleep/waking → consider sleep apnea, cluster headache, or hypnic headache before assuming migraine. Ask about timing precision, autonomic symptoms (tearing, runny nose), and attack duration.
