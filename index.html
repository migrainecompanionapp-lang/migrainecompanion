What Makes Us Different

Most migraine apps are digital diaries. MIgraine Companion takes a fundamentally different approach — one rooted in narrative therapy.

🌙 Externalization

Mi represents your migraine as something separate from you. You're not "a migraine sufferer" — you're a person who lives with Mi. This reduces self-blame and increases your sense of control.

💜 Companionization

Instead of fighting your migraine, you learn to understand it. Mi is not a villain to defeat. When you struggle, Mi struggles too. You're companions, not adversaries.

✨ Your Actions Matter

Every action has a visible effect. Drink water — Mi calms down. Rest — Mi relaxes. Skip a meal — Mi grows restless. You see the direct connection between self-care and your condition.

💫 Sparkling Moments

Your calm days aren't just statistics — they're proof that you can manage this. Each peaceful day is a "sparkling moment," evidence that the problem doesn't always win.

🛏️ Crisis Support

When migraine strikes, Mi doesn't demand anything. Mi sleeps beside you, sends gentle reminders, asks how you're feeling. The app becomes comfort, not pressure.

Based on narrative therapy developed by Michael White and David Epston — a scientifically validated approach used for chronic pain, depression, and anxiety for over 40 years. You don't need a therapist to benefit. Mi is with you, in your pocket, ready whenever you need.

✦ The Breakthrough

The Prodrome Window

Hours before pain hits, your body whispers.
Mi listens.

Mi reads 9 biomarkers from your Apple Watch — HRV, heart rate, resting HR, respiratory rate, blood oxygen, wrist temperature, sleep stages, sleep duration, activity. First, Mi learns your baseline — what's normal for YOUR body. Then, with each migraine you log, Mi learns how your body shifts before pain hits — your unique migraine signature. The pattern starts forming after 5 logged migraines; accuracy sharpens dramatically after 15+. When that signature emerges again, the Prodrome Window opens.

Universal safety tracking

Regardless of attack frequency, these features work for every user — built into Mi from day one.

💊
MOH Prevention

Medication limit tracking prevents medication overuse headache — the most common but invisible trap for frequent users (triptans >10 days/month, gepants >15 days/month).

📊
CGRP Effectiveness

Before/after tracking for CGRP therapy (Aimovig, Ajovy, Emgality, Vyepti, Ubrelvy, Nurtec) — see what's actually working, share the data with your neurologist.

🩺
Specialist Support

Mi is not a replacement for medical care. If you have 15+ attacks per month, please work with a headache specialist — Mi complements, never replaces.

Built from 30 Years of Living with Migraine

A letter from the creator

I've lived with migraines for 30 years. I built MIgraine Companion not as a developer — but as someone who needed it to exist.

This app was created in Ukraine during wartime, often with only 8 hours of electricity per day. Sometimes less. In those dark hours — both literal and metaphorical — I worked on something that would bring light to others who live with chronic pain.

🌙 Personal Experience

30 years living with chronic migraine. Reduced attacks from 15-20 per month to 3-4 using the same therapeutic approach built into this app.

📚 Evidence-Based

Built on narrative therapy principles by Michael White & David Epston. Uses WHO medication guidelines and medical research on migraine triggers.

✨ Transparency

Rustam is not a medical doctor. This app is a self-help tool based on personal experience and research — it supplements, not replaces, professional medical care.

💜 Built in Wartime

Developed in Ukraine during 2024-2026. Often working through 10-16 hour power outages. Created with hope that understanding can help others.

I didn't know I was building a narrative therapy tool. I was building what I wished existed — something that would make me feel less alone, less defined by my condition, more in control.

Mi isn't just a cute character. Mi is how I learned to live with my migraine — not as an enemy to fight, but as a companion to understand.
